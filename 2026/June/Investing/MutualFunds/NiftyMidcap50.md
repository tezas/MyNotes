## 0. CONTEXT — Midcap 150 is a different RISK asset (not just another index)
- **Nifty Midcap 150 = companies ranked 101–250** by market cap. Higher growth potential, MUCH higher volatility.
- **~50%+ drawdowns possible** in crashes (vs ~38% for Nifty 50). Can stay down for years.
- **Tracking error structurally wider** than Nifty 50 (~0.1–0.4% vs 0.03%) — midcaps are less liquid, harder to replicate. Fund choice matters more.
- **Allocation decision (per user):** reduced from 25% → **15%** of the ₹2L SIP (₹30k/mo). Risk trimmed while keeping a growth tilt.
- **Approach (per user):** INDEX fund, not active (active midcaps beat the index more often than large-caps, but still a minority and you pay 1%+; indexing is the disciplined low-maintenance choice).

⚠️ **Star ratings & 1Y returns are USELESS here:** all 7 funds show 3★ and ~5.3–5.6% 1Y return — identical, because they hold the same basket. Decision = tracking difference + tracking error + TER, exactly as before.

---

## 1. DECISION POINTS
1. **Tracking Difference (Direct)** — net return lag vs Nifty Midcap 150 TRI. #1 metric. Expect wider (more negative) than Nifty 50 — normal for the category.
2. **Tracking Error** — consistency of replication. Higher absolute values than Nifty 50 expected.
3. **TER** — ⚠️ Excel shows BASE expense ratio (understated); verify all-in on factsheet.
4. **AUM gate** ~₹500 Cr+ (liquidity matters more for midcaps).
5. **Track record / pedigree** = tiebreaker.
6. **Direct + Growth only.**

**Decision rule:** Among adequately-sized funds, least-negative tracking difference (Direct) wins; ties broken on TER, then record.

## 3. CONCLUSION & REASON

**Status: ALL 7 verified ✅. Motilal & Nippon co-leaders; SBI close 3rd (fee); ABSL 4th (fee); ICICI/Navi behind; HDFC mid-pack.**

### 🏆 FINAL DECISION: Motilal Oswal Nifty Midcap 150 Index (Direct-Growth) — narrow lean; Nippon equally valid co-pick
- **Motilal & Nippon tied on net return** (≈−0.33% vs −0.34%, within noise). Motilal wins the low-noise metrics: lowest TER (0.21%), largest AUM (₹3,589 Cr, matters most in less-liquid midcaps), tightest TE among leaders (0.07%), longest single-manager record (Swapnil Mayekar since 2019).
- **Nippon = equally sound co-pick**: its tracking difference is a *reported/computed Direct* figure (Motilal's is *estimated* from Regular), and it has no exit load (Motilal: 1% if ≤15d, trivial long-term). Choose Nippon if you weight data-reliability over Motilal's TER/AUM/record edge.
- SBI (−0.38%) & ABSL (−0.43%): good tracking but higher fees (0.34–0.40%) hold them back.
- ICICI tightest TE of all (0.06%) yet −0.61% net — 5th time low TE ≠ best net return.

⚠️ ICICI checked: tightest TE of all (0.06%) but 1Y tracking difference −0.61% — tied with Navi at the BACK. Proof again: low TE ≠ best net return.
⚠️ SBI checked: −0.38% net (close to leaders) but TER 0.40% total is highest of contenders → fee makes it less attractive than Motilal/Nippon despite good tracking.
⚠️ HDFC checked: TER 0.27% (higher than leaders), tracking difference not printed → mid-pack at best, no path to #1.

**Observations:**
- **Nippon (−0.34%, reported) and Motilal (≈−0.33%, estimated) are effectively tied** on net return — within rounding/estimation noise.
- **Motilal's edges:** tightest tracking error (0.07% vs Nippon 0.14%), slightly lower TER (0.21% vs 0.25%), largest AUM (₹3,589 Cr), and a since-inception (2019) single manager (Swapnil Mayekar) — longest, most stable record in the category. ⚠️ BUT its tracking difference is *estimated* from Regular-plan figures (+0.69% fee adj), not a reported Direct number — and it carries a **1% exit load if redeemed ≤15 days** (trivial for long-term).
- **Nippon's edges:** tracking difference is a *reported/computed Direct* figure (more reliable than Motilal's estimate); no exit load.
- **Navi (−0.61%)** clearly behind despite tightest-but-one TE — low TE ≠ best net return, again.

**LEAN: Motilal Oswal Nifty Midcap 150 Index — narrowly.** Tightest tracking error (the most reliable forward indicator for an index fund), lowest TER, biggest AUM, longest single-manager record. Its estimated tracking difference ties Nippon's. **Nippon is the equally-valid co-pick / safer choice** if you prefer a *reported* (non-estimated) tracking difference and no exit load.

⚠️ This is a genuine near-tie (like Next 50), NOT a clear win like Nifty 50's Nippon. Either Motilal or Nippon is a sound choice.

**Optional next step:** verify ICICI (0.22 TER) & HDFC (0.23) — but both would need to beat a ~−0.33% tracking difference to change the top, and neither has Motilal's TE/AUM/record edge. SBI & ABSL (0.34 TER) start with a fee disadvantage.
