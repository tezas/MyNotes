# Nifty Next 50 Index Fund — Selection Document

## 0. WHY NEXT 50 IS DIFFERENT (read first)
- **Nifty Next 50 = stocks ranked 51–100** (the "on-deck" large caps / aspiring blue-chips). NOT the same companies as Nifty 50.
- **More volatile, harder to track:** higher index turnover (stocks graduate in/out of Nifty 50), lower liquidity → **tracking error & tracking difference are STRUCTURALLY WIDER than Nifty 50 funds.** Expect TE ~0.2–0.5% (vs ~0.03% for Nifty 50). This is normal for the category, not a fund defect.
- Because tracking is harder, **the spread between good and bad funds is wider here** → fund choice matters MORE than for Nifty 50.
- ⚠️ The same evaluation order applies: **Tracking Difference > Tracking Error > TER**, AUM as a gate.

---

## 1. DECISION POINTS
1. **Tracking Difference (Direct)** = net return lag vs Nifty Next 50 TRI. #1 metric. Less negative = better.
2. **Tracking Error** = consistency of tracking. Expect higher absolute values than Nifty 50.
3. **TER** = sticker fee. ⚠️ Excel TER understated for Nifty 50 funds (verify on factsheet) — same risk here.
4. **AUM gate** ~₹1,000 Cr+. Several Next 50 funds are sub-scale (₹9–270 Cr) → liquidity/tracking risk is real here, more than for Nifty 50.
5. **Track record / pedigree** = tiebreaker.
6. **Exit load / turnover** = minor; noted.
7. **Direct + Growth only.**

**Decision rule:** Among adequately-sized funds, least-negative tracking difference (Direct) wins; break ties on TER, then record.

**TIGHT CLUSTER at top — no runaway winner (unlike Nifty 50's Nippon).** Top 4 funds span just −0.18% to −0.38%, mostly within noise.
- **DSP** has best *estimated* net return but it's NOT a reported Direct figure (Regular +0.56% fee adj) → less certain.
- **Edelweiss** −0.32% is genuinely good & reported, BUT: TER is 0.22% all-in (NOT the 0.07% base the Excel showed — not the cheap outlier expected), widest TE (0.18%), smallest AUM (₹256 Cr), data 1mo stale.
- **SBI** −0.36% reported, tightest TE (0.0345%), good AUM, but 0.50%/30d exit load + 0.31% all-in TER.
- **Navi** −0.38% reported, CHEAPEST all-in (0.15%), no exit load, good AUM.

**FINAL CALL: Navi Nifty Next 50 Index Fund (Direct-Growth)** — best balance of the durable, certain factor (lowest all-in fee 0.15%), a reported (not estimated) competitive tracking difference (−0.38%), adequate AUM, and no exit load. The funds ahead of it on raw tracking difference each carry a real offsetting caveat (DSP=estimated; Edelweiss=tiny AUM+widest TE+stale; SBI=higher fee+exit load). For a long-term hold, the lowest certain fee with reported competitive tracking is the soundest bet.
**Co-pick / equally valid: SBI** (if you prefer largest tracking precision + scale and don't mind the exit load). **DSP** worth choosing only if its Direct tracking difference is confirmed.

⚠️ Still unverified: Axis (0.13), Motilal (0.26), ABSL (0.30) — all priced at/above Navi, so they face the same fee-drag headwind; unlikely to beat Navi.
