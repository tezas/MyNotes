## 1. DECISION POINTS

How a Nifty 50 index fund is judged (all funds hold the *identical* 50 stocks, so stock-picking is irrelevant — only execution + cost differ):

1. **Tracking Difference is the #1 metric** = how much the fund's NET return lags the Nifty 50 TRI. Less negative = more return you actually keep. This already includes fees, so it's the truest measure.
2. **Tracking Error is #2** = how *consistently* the fund hugs the index (volatility of the gap). Low = mechanically precise. ⚠️ But low tracking error ≠ best return — SBI/HDFC track tightest yet deliver less because their fees are higher.
3. **TER is #3** = the sticker fee. Lower is better, but it's already captured inside tracking difference. ⚠️ The Excel TER was consistently *understated* vs live factsheets (UTI 0.18→0.23, SBI 0.16→0.19, Kotak 0.06→0.07, HDFC 0.22→0.26) — always trust the factsheet.
4. **AUM is a gate, not a ranker** = above ~₹1,000 Cr, size stops mattering. All top funds clear this; bigger ≠ better net return.
5. **Track record / AMC pedigree** = tiebreaker. A longer, proven tracking history (e.g. since 2010) beats a brand-new fund with no data, even at equal cost.
6. **Exit load & turnover** = minor for a long-term holder; noted for completeness.
7. **Direct plan + Growth option only** (never Regular, never IDCW).

**Decision rule:** Among funds with adequate AUM, pick the **least-negative tracking difference (Direct plan)**; break ties on **TER**, then **track record**.

## 3. CONCLUSION & REASON

### 🏆 WINNER: **Nippon India Index Fund – Nifty 50 Plan (Direct-Growth)**

**Reason:**
1. **Best net return delivered** — tracking difference −0.10%(1Y)/−0.22%(3Y)/−0.25%(5Y) is the least-negative of every fund verified. It loses the least of the index's return to you, at every horizon.
2. **Cheapest fee (tied)** — TER 0.06%, lowest in the category alongside Navi.
3. **Tight tracking** — tracking error 0.03% (excellent; <0.2% is great).
4. **Ample AUM** — ₹3,662 Cr, well past the adequacy gate.
5. **Long, proven record** — running since 2010, far longer than the newer low-cost rivals.
6. **Zero exit load, very low turnover (0.10×).**

**Why not the others:**
- **Navi (#2 / fallback):** ties on cost (0.06%), tracks marginally *tighter* (TE 0.02%), bigger AUM — genuinely excellent. But its tracking difference (−0.20%) is worse than Nippon's (−0.10%), and it's a newer AMC (since 2021). The best alternative if you prefer it.
- **Kotak (#3):** matches on cost but tracking error (0.06%) is 2× Nippon's, and its Direct tracking difference is only estimated (factsheet printed Regular).
- **SBI / UTI / HDFC / ICICI:** all track the index tightly (SBI tightest of all at 0.0158% TE) but their **higher fees drag net return below Nippon's** — proving tracking difference, not tracking error, is the right arbiter.
- **JioBlackRock & other Tier-3 (Zerodha, Choice, Angel, Bajaj, Groww):** too new / sub-scale / no verified tracking data — cannot be evaluated; watch, don't buy.
- **High-fee funds (LIC 0.47, Taurus 0.66, Franklin 0.24):** disqualified on cost alone.

### Key lesson from this analysis
**Low tracking error ≠ best returns.** SBI and HDFC hug the index most precisely, but their higher fees mean they deliver *less* net return than the cheaper Nippon. Always rank on **tracking difference (net of fees)**, not tracking error or sticker TER alone — and always verify TER on the live factsheet (the Excel understated it for every fund).

⚠️ Caveats held honestly: Kotak's Direct TrkDiff is estimated (+0.26% fee adjustment on Regular figures); Bandhan's Direct TrkDiff estimated (+0.43% fee adj); Navi's −0.20% period is unlabelled; ICICI's live TER and HDFC's TrkDiff weren't printed in their docs. None of these gaps change the ranking — Nippon leads in every reading.
