
### Fundamental Research

#### P/E
Price per Earning ratio : Price of a stock / (Earnings per share) 
Means: 
- If P/E is x then you pay x₹ to gain 1₹ profit.
- Also means it will take x years of earning to get back the money you're paying.

Example:
- If we buy a flat for 60 lakh(i.e., price) and rent for the flat is 1lakh/month(12 lakh / year i.e., EPS) then it would take 5 years to get back the money which means P/E of flat is 5.

Theory:
- The price reflects what the market expects about the future, but earnings (E) is usually from the past (last 2 months).
- A high P/E means investors are happy to pay a lot for today's ₹1 of profit — usually because they expect that profit to grow a lot. If the company is growing fast, a high P/E can be justified.
- A low P/E can mean the stock is genuinely cheap... or that the business is in trouble and the market expects profits
  to shrink.
