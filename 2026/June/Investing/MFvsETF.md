## 3. THE TER-vs-SPREAD TRADE-OFF (the crux)

ETF's headline win is the lower TER (~0.04% vs ~0.06%). BUT:
- That ~0.02% fee saving is **tiny**.
- A thin ETF's **bid-ask spread (0.5–2%)** on entry/exit can erase *years* of that saving in a single trade.
- Only the **most liquid** Nifty 50 ETFs have spreads tight enough (~0.01–0.05%) for the TER edge to actually survive.
- The mutual fund has **zero spread** — you always transact at NAV.

➡️ **For regular/small transactions, the MF's clean NAV usually beats the ETF's lower TER. For large one-shot trades in a highly liquid ETF, the ETF wins.**

---

## 4. NEUTRAL VERDICT

> Same engine (Nifty 50 TRI), different wrapper. **ETF = lower fee + intraday control + tax-efficient (abroad), but needs demat, has spreads, and can't auto-SIP cleanly. Mutual Fund = clean NAV + native SIP + fractional + no demat, at a marginally higher fee.**

- **Neither is "better" absolutely.** The ETF is the more *efficient* vehicle in a vacuum (lower cost); the mutual fund is the more *practical* vehicle for disciplined regular investing in India.
- **Globally**, ETFs have largely won for passive investing — but mainly because abroad they're deeply liquid AND tax-advantaged, neither of which fully holds in India.
